Building a Fashion Game

Dolli is an ultra-girly, nostalgic artistic outlet: a 2D game about creative self-expression through fashion and character customization. I'm building Dolli solo! Every asset is hand-drawn by me in Krita, and the demo is coded from scratch in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. The idea started back in 2025 as a way to revive my Figma skills, but I quickly realized I wanted this to be an actual game. I've had a lot of free time since February, so I began building a playable webpage demo.

How I Started.

I started on paper by sketching the doll's shape and the layout of the game. The sketches were imported into Krita, and I just started drawing a bunch of different hairstyles, eye shapes, clothes, skin tones, buttons, etc.

Three of each facial feature and clothing option were enough for me to start building the prototype in Figma. I used the sketch idea for the initial layout, which was originally built for desktop platforms.

Platform Switch

But don’t I want my game to be available offline? I especially wanted my game to be available to kids who may not have their own computer to use. My assumption was that most kids would use their tablet or phone to play games. So instead of building a desktop web game prototype, I pivoted to designing a mobile layout for iOS and Android. The game would also be available cross-platform, mobile and web.

One decision I'm proud of: features drawn once don't look right on every skin tone, and a lot of dress-up games treat darker skin as an afterthought. So I drew separate eye and nose variants for lighter and darker complexions, and the demo swaps them automatically as you change skin tones.

Under the hood, the demo already has a full undo system, hue and opacity sliders, and lets you save, name, and reload up to five Dollis. Playing dress-up is easy; making dress-up feel effortless took some real state management.

The initial idea was to create a simple fashion game, but I know it'll grow into so much more. I need to figure out how to make Dolli fun as I want anyone who plays to want to come back. My rough ideas are noted, but I don't want to fly too close to the sun just yet. For now, I'm getting started on the basics of Godot. More posts soon!
