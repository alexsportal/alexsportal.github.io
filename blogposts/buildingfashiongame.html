Building a Fashion Game

I’ll try to keep this as short as I can. Dolli is an ultra-girly and nostalgic artistic outlet. This is an idea I started back in 2025. I initially started this to revive my Figma skills...but I quickly realized I wanted this to be an actual game. I’ve had a lot of free time since February this year, so I began building a simple webpage demo.

Try Demo

I did this first because:

  1. I’m not familiar with Godot yet.
  2. I want to see how potential gameplayers would enjoy something like this.

The demo just one part of the game; the avatar creation (aka the beauty parlor).

Click to see concept overview.

How I Started.

I started on paper by sketching the doll’s shape and the layout of the game. The sketches were imported into Krita, and I just started drawing a bunch of different hairstyles, eye shapes, clothes, skin tones, buttons, etc.

Three of each facial feature/clothing options were enough for me to start building the prototype in Figma. I used the sketch idea for the initial layout which was supposed to be built for desktop platforms.

Platform Switch

But don’t I want my game to be available offline? I especially wanted my game to be available to kids who may not have their own computer to use. My assumption was that most kids would use their tablet or phone to play games. So instead of building a desktop web game prototype, I pivoted to designing a mobile layout for iOS and Android. The game would also be available cross-platform, mobile and web.
